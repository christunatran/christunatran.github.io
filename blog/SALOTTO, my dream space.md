i visited the [Salotto](https://salotto.nyc/) space today to attend a talk by Giorgia Lupi. i found the concept of the space so inspiring. 

to my understanding, it's a coworking space shared by a few small design firms and hosts events. while it serves a functional business purpose to keep itself afloat, it keeps a cozy atmosphere. fitting, since "salotto" in italian translates to "living room". this is felt with the big cloud couch in the center stage. 

my boyfriend often says something along the lines of, "our brains fill the space of the room we're in". i could see myself being my most creative and doing my best work in a space like this. when i spend my time in creative spaces and surrounded by creative people, i find my mind generating my wildest ideas. i love this feeling. 

at the event today with matt and arjun, all of a sudden i want to start a podcast with A, to have my apartment double as a coworking space welcoming friends and collaborators, to make intentionally bad paintings, to finish every night with a glass of funky wine like an italian in a "wet culture". 

now, i'm thinking about what i need to do to run my own salotto. i've visited offices that feel cozy, like ambrook. index is nice but still feels very much like an office. verci is a space i used to frequent but maybe its overly ambitious and young vibe is not exactly the cozy, mature, and chill that i'm looking for especially at this stage in my twenties. merlin's place at fractal is someone's home.

i often think about how i can use and share my privilege in a way that uplifts others; to share the opportunities and resources that i have access to. privileges, or unfair advantages i have right now: being a student at NYU and having lived in NYC for the past 3+ years. a start has been hosting this "stupid hackathon" and trying to open up the NYU resources to the wider community, and sharing events that i'm privy to to those new to NYC or the art/tech space. 

but post-grad, it really would be nice to have this home/studio space to share. maybe once my lease is up this fall, i'll look for like-minded roommate(s) and pitch the idea of an open intimate community "living room" open for people to come use as a third space and/or do work in. just hanging out!!! chatting and letting ideas wander freely. i think that would be so nice, especially in post-grad when everyone goes their separate ways. it doesn't have to be that way! and then end the evenings with dinner and a glass of wine together. doesn't that sound so nice?
