i tracked that i spent almost 2 hours on my full night routine tonight… 
went on a short run,
took a shower,
flossed,
put on hasan minhaj’s podcast,
brushed my teeth,
did my skincare,
blow dried my hair. 

does everyone do this? how is this sustainable? getting ready and un-ready, hair, makeup, outfits are parts that i love and hate about being a woman. hate because it takes so much TIME to be “put together”. why is it that collectively society is conditioned to have lower appearance standards for men..? i don’t have it in me to put on makeup everyday. every time i witness my boyfriend shower, brush his teeth, and just roll into bed, i feel a tinge of irritation :/

i’d probably feel even more terrible if i didn’t fill the silence while doing skincare and blow drying my hair with a podcast or audiobook. i’ve tried embracing the silence and watching videos but 1) i feel like i spend so much time in my day deliberately being bored or in silence that it’s fine to use this time for "fun enrichment" lol 2) it feels like just the right amount of stimulation to be doing stuff with my hands while listening with my ears. 

and now it’s 10:51pm and i have to wake up at 8:00am and so i have about an hour left to myself before i have to go to bed if i want to get a full 8 hours of sleep.

i’ve been thinking about this idea of “mourning time” ever since i stumbled upon a tiktok (haha) of someone talking about it. how we mourn our “lost” time before it’s even arrived. 

what that looks like for me is thinking about the limited time left i have to do things that gives me a powerful urge to just give up and lie flat. and often it wins. i pick up the handy dandy tool that is my phone and swipe around the flashy notifications and icons and apps and drown away the negative feelings. is this nihilism?

reading 4000 weeks has led me to view the fleeting of time as something positive rather than negative. i feel less afraid of my own mortality, and that to live the most meaningful life i have to confront that mortality. 

as they say… 

> it’s not that deep

the time will pass anyway. i’d rather stop giving it so much thought. i’d like to start ignoring its passage and existence, and to just DO THE THINGS. because 

> the time will pass anyway
