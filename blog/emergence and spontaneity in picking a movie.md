chris is my mom's good friend and housemate. he's a (horror) movie enthusiast, and over the past few years of coexistence, has become an unlikely friend of mine too. he watches at least 1 movie a day, so it's always interesting to walk past the living room and peek at what he has playing on the TV. 

today, i sat back and decided to watch whatever he picked. we ended up watching "A Desert" (2024), which was... a freaky movie that i otherwise would've never chosen nor finished on my own. despite not enjoying the story, i gained inspiration from the movie's unique cinematography choices. 

it got me thinking about welcoming emergence and spontaneity into my everyday life. i relinquished control and welcomed chris's input for the media i consume, which opened up a new, positive experience for me. 

i tend to delude myself that the most optimal way to work, learn, grow, and exist is to follow my own routine toward planned goals. that the chaos that other people bring into my life just gives me anxiety and distractions, and that it should be compartmentalized and not be allowed to overly influence me. but i'm now seeing that this wasn't a good way to look at it. 

i'm now reminded of the Oblique Strategies card deck designed to disrupt habitual, outcome-based thinking, and to encourage introduce randomness and the unexpected into the process. in this case, chris, and other people, help me to break my initial plan for how to spend my time, and welcoming their randomness and unexpectedness helped me to reach an outcome that i otherwise wouldn't have alone.

in my "Innovation at Speed" class, every class, we were given 90 minutes to prepare a presentation in groups. one day, the topic surrounded how to welcome "open innovation" into our process, which led our instructor to swap 1 member from each group 30 minutes into the preparation time. in my experience, at a crucial moment where the team would already be set on an agreed idea, having a new team member join midway forced us to quickly re-explain and validate our idea and gave a fresh perspective to develop our idea further.

like with individual cards in the Oblique Strategies deck, each person that we welcome into our "process" or our lives brings with them their own randomness, and its important to welcome them even when it may feel counterintuitive, to reach unexpected outcomes that we wouldn't otherwise alone.

> "we can go faster alone but further together"

this year, in a professional sense, i'm hoping that i can work on more collaborative projects. in a fun sense, i'm hoping to spend more time with people to experience their perspective and interesting outcomes from their influence, within a reasonable extent. life is more fun with other humans!
