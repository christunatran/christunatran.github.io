when you know in your heart that you’re going to take a nap later in the day, it makes it a lot easier to wake up in the morning.

maybe what america needs is more *siesta time*.

when i was in vietnam, relatives and friends would be knocking on my door as early as 6am to just… hang out. straight to coffee and breakfast. we’d all go about our days and then at around mid-afternoon they’d drop me off at home to collectively take a nap, instruct me to take a shower in preparation to continue hanging out once the sun lets out in the evening.

when i’m in new york city i often wake up feeling so exhausted and stretched thin from all of the things going on in my daily life. it feels like i, or those around me can hardly get enough sleep every night. and school and work doesn’t have places to nap or rest, making me feel like i can’t rest until everything is done at the very end of the day, once i finally get to my home. on top of this, there is a trend here to follow an ideal morning routine consisting of making your own coffee and breakfast, taking a cold shower, meditating, morning pages, etc. etc. i now find this unsustainable. all of these expectations just create more friction each morning on top of the lack of sleep. it often results in some form of morning bedtime procrastination where i try to sleep in as much as i possibly can before i absolutely have to wake up. this often ends up in giving myself under 30 minutes to get ready in the morning, making it even more unsustainable to commit to that elaborate routine that’s supposed to set up my day for success.

but somehow, when there’s a nap to look forward to in the middle of the day, i can embark on my day knowing that there is a certain end to this tiredness; there’s rest in sight, and it’s not necessarily an entire day away.

i’m building non-negotiable naps into my days now.
