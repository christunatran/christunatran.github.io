a thought i had: what is stopping us from treating our entire lives as a big performance art?

fear? of what? of not being well-received? of totally casting away societal norms and ending up broke on the streets as the result?
 
i think a lot of human desires boil down to just wanting to feel loved.

in this context, maybe its our emotional attachment to our identities that we've built up so far? again, fear of change and the unknown?

i guess we're all performing in some form when we present ourselves in the society we live in. 

but what would it look like to detach and apply some more intention to who we *want* to be in this moment? 

not much is stopping me from going buck-wild and giving into all of my intrusive impulses (i have quite a lot) in a given moment. though, they're not that crazy, and i think i get most of the energy out through stimming. though, i wonder if it would look different if i simply *allow* myself to express my desires and obtain them in an unobstructed way. i think stimming is inherently repressed, because i'm letting the energy out, but in a way that isn't overly disruptive to those around me. and i think that habit bleeds into how i may stim when i am in private in my room.

am i repressed, or am i convincing myself that i am repressed? because i'm not lashing out like characters i see on TV?
