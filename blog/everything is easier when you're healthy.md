uhhhh, no brainer here. but i find it really true, especially now that i'm being more intentional about prioritizing my health this year.

what that looks like: prioritizing sleep, not skipping meals, exercising regularly-- making sure to train my back/upper body.

i used to experience a lot of back pain from commuting with my backpack every day, and thought it was inescapable. i tried daily stretching and yoga and lightening my bag, but nothing was more effective than simply training my back with weights. now the back pain is not even a thought that occurs to my mind. having a stronger back (and core) has subconsciously improved my posture too.

i used to skip sleep, meals, and gym days in favor of getting more work done. i have a bad habit of white-knuckling through unpleasant things. but when i take care of myself, i'm more focused and effective while i'm doing my tasks... often times, i get them done faster. vietnamese people care a lot about "resting well", and what that can look like varies from person-to-person, even day-to-day. sometimes i need to just lay down, close my eyes, and without sleeping, just do...nothing. sometimes it's having a laugh with a friend. other times it's bedrotting on my phone. whatever quality rest looks like that day. rest gives me more energy throughout the day. it 'extends' my day, not that that's exactly the goal though.

working out is an act of revolution?
