a wise man once said, "rich people don't have sauce."

"...so they buy sauce from other people", in the form of art, clothing, music, and other materialistic pursuits.

some of the funniest people i know are those i grew up with in the public school system. once i get to the wealthier suburbs in the DMV, or yuppies in NYC, i struggle to muster a laugh. sure, i acknowledge things are funny, but i rarely find myself laughing out loud the same way. i failed to master the art of pretending to laugh at rich people humor during my days in corporate. it's hard to laugh at dad joke after dad joke interjected between conversations about skiing, golf, wine, summmers in europe and israel and stuff like that. 

lol look at me being acting all morally superior.

if you grew up going to private schools, and spend most of your time around people in the same tax bracket as you, you're probably not very funny outside of your bubble. sorry...
