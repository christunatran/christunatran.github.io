this show made me believe in love again.

it’s broken and mended my heart over and over again.

i think i’ve cried enough tears to fill the pacific ocean tonight…

gwan-shik and ae-sun seem to have the world against them, from the patriarchy and gender roles, extended family, in-laws, financial hardship, grief, and even sacrificing what they thought were their individual dreams to be together… and yet when they’re together they’re able to endure it all… everything began to come together for them when they stopped denying themselves from one another, and they built a beautiful life together. (i think the show is based on a true story too.)

i wonder if i’ll experience this kind of love in my lifetime. 

it’s so touching how gwan-shik always defends ae-sun from the world and the demands of his family. he’s always on her side. it reminds me of my own family, where my dad wasn’t able to do that for my mom. it’s surprising how vietnam is so progressive these days as a country, but the refugees who fled post-war 70s-90s seem to live in the same version of vietnam they left within America. where a husband who comes to their wife’s aide is seen as a radical act enough to be vilified and viewed as an unfilial son. i love gwan-shik.

coincidentally my family comes from a fishing village as well. there’s just too many parallels, even though this show is from a totally different country in asia. and yet my t-shirt is now filled with snot because i couldn’t be bothered to get up and find tissues.

if you care to understand me then i urge you to watch “when life gives you tangerines”.
