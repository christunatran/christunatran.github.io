we've reached a peak in hyperrealistic AI images... now it's time to take steps backwards [In Defense of the Poor Image](https://www.e-flux.com/journal/10/61362/in-defense-of-the-poor-image).

it's interesting how intentionally "crunching down" the quality of images/videos shot on modern cameras to resemble old-school camcorder-esque has become a trendy aesthetic.

constraints are great. maybe we can put constraints on our video quality to focus more on *the story being told*... in favor of doing *more* with *less*.

i'm thinking about john wilson, who walks around NYC with a camera and compiles the footage into intimate, comedic, chaotic documentary-style narrative pieces.
