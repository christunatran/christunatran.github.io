on halloween this year, i went to a party thrown by people who would typically be labeled as "theatre kids". i am writing to report that as adults, theatre kids seem to have the most fun.

these folks really know how to throw down. the party was called "monster house", and had a 2-hour show full of dance and other performance acts to kick it off. the event had a narrative that the emcees consistently incorporated, there was a stage with professional, hired AV staff working it, there was even lighting design within the house windows. people in elaborate costumes that clearly looked like they took weeks or longer of thought and effort to hand-make. i can't articulate it fully with my limited writing ability... you just had to be there. 

in my shyness, i felt really out of place, but somehow in a good way. i more or less fell into an observer-role... and i did have a hoot observing the human experience that night.

i mainly just couldn't believe there were people willing to put in so much effort *for a house party*... the attention to detail was inspiring, to say the least... it made me think about when i forgot the value of putting effort into things that are just *fun* rather than only allowing it for things that may move my career forward. in personal and professional contexts i tend to struggle with the last 10% that makes a project polished.

it reminded me of what Hal, who works at Art of Play (a toy store in downtown brooklyn that also blew my mind), said: "many people think that magic is only for kids, but that's wrong... adults are the ones who need magic the most, to remember what it's like to play"... to have fun... to wonder... to *try*, to *care*!!! a (valid) complaint i often hear online these days is about how it has become "cringe" to "care too much" or "try too hard". the people who adhere to that are definitely not having fun (i am guilty of it too).

i love watching humans just *be humans*. makes me also wonder... when did i fall into this stiff, timid version of myself, where i subconsciously repress my true thoughts and expression? am i trying to conform? (though not succeeding at that either).

why does it take so much now for me to open up my shell and actually be myself? only a few have been welcome to witness this. i wish it didn't. i don't mean to be this way. i'm not a people-hater. several people have likened me to a robot in my life, and frankly it's not something i am happy to hear. :/

lots of questions i'm asking myself.

play... i remember in early spring of this year, Franky invited me to her neighborhood to just "play". i was shook. play??? so vague, but sure. we ended up wandering around the neighborhood and through prospect park with no real plan in mind. just hanging out with each other and sharing our observations and interacting with what was directly around us. PLAY!

anyways, i had a lot of fun. i think something in me is changing. i'm gradually embracing play again.
