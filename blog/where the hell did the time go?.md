it's already december 11, the last day of classes of my first semester of grad school. where did the time go? 

in my 9-6 corporate job, i felt stuck doing the same thing day-in and day-out but it gave me something to look forward to in the activities i would sprinkle in after (and sometimes during) the work day. delaying the gratification of my post-work activities and having the memories i wanted to hold onto be so sparse made them stand out more in my memory.

in my "adult gap year" the days felt short because of the lack of structure that encouraged me to explore, but made it difficult to commit to anything besides being overcompensatingly hedonistic to reward myself for the past consecutive years of studying and working. there were lots of moments where it was so incredibly boring and moments overflowing with experiences and perspective-shifts... but the lack of tangible outputs made that time pass without leaving a big mark.

here, in this "art school for engineers", there is obvious yet non-traditional structure, for example, in how i have my class and part-time work schedule, but most begin after 12pm, which is when i usually arrive. this was a big transition from taking 8am classes in undergrad or clocking into work at 9am sharp. i typically treat school like a normal 8-hour work day, staying on "The Floor", what we colloquially refer to as the location of our school department, until at least 8pm. there's so much happening before and after those hours; it makes 1 day feel like 3. 

at a bare minimum daily schedule looks like this:
- 9am get out of bed
- 11am arrive at school
- 12pm-3pm class
- 3pm-8pm stick around at school for another class, meetings, do work
- 9pm arrive home
- 12am go to bed

i try to squeeze in time to:
- socialize (lots of yapping on The Floor)
- go to the gym
- take breaks, nap
- occasionally go to dance class
- go to local events 1-2 times a week (guilty of going to many more earlier in the semester)

as much as i aspired to get to school early and treat it like a 9-5, i found that in art school, or at least this program, things skew later in the day. since most of the community stays later rather than wake up early, i naturally fell into that routine. it wasn't unusual during crunch times to stay as late as 10, 11, 12, and one time, even 3am.

this place makes me concurrently feel like i have so much and so little time. if i sleep for 8 hours, then it leaves me with 16 hours, at least 2-3 hours getting ready/unready and commuting, and the rest almost fully consumed by school. there isn’t even time to doom-scroll (this is mostly good). during that time, i'm constantly bombarded with new information, people, and work. i'm pushed to keep going and going, unable to catch my breath, until i force myself across this semester's finish line. but i'm so exhausted and numbed out at this point to even feel the relief.

i look back at the work i made this semester and there are lots of moments where a project could've been a lot better had i spent more time developing the idea, asking for help, or being more patient with building it carefully. i thought that i had been spending so much time at school, but maybe a lot of it was face-time rather than effective-time? am i deluding myself, and i actually squandered all of the time i had by not prioritizing better? have i been overestimating the amount of time i have, only to suffer the consequences of only feeling urgency in the face of an impending deadline? to cut the self-critique short, i still grew a lot this semester. several people told me to give myself grace for this adjustment period of returning to school. my anxiety comes from wanting results and not seeing them right away... but exactly what this adjustment period required was *time*. and for me, i guess that was the past 3.5 months. better later and longer than never... it's only up from here.

a notoriously anti-school acquaintance (so much so that he started his own alternative school) conceded that he likes school because for many "it's the only time you fully invest in yourself", and i truly feel that right now. it's a privilege to drop everything and have long, tiring days dedicated to learning and personal, artistic, and intellectual growth. at the end of the day, it beats my corporate job where my personal and technical growth plateaued. this may be the most meaningful time of my life thus far, and the growing pains serve as proof of it.

so, with my sense of time and feeling so messed up, i'm not surprised that between the time that i wake up and return home, it feels like 3 days have passed in 1.
